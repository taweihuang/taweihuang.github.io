About

I’ll start as a Ph.D. student in Quantitative Marketing at Harvard Business School in 2020 Fall. I graduated from National Tsing Hua University with a BS in Quantitative Finance and completed my MS in Statistics from National Taiwan University.

I am broadly interested in causal inference and machine learning in the field of marketing, especially topics in customer lifecycle management and policy measurement. My goal is to combine economic models and modern data science techniques to understand the heterogeneity behind customer decisions and how firms should develop strategies to fulfill customers’ needs.

I had 3-year industry experience as a data scientist and analytic consultant with project experience in business experimentation, new product introduction, personalized recommendation, customer lifecycle management, and public policy. I’ve worked for clients and companies in retail, restaurant, consumer electronics, commercial banking, and internet sectors in Greater China and Southeast Asia.
